This file is intentionally a small, public-domain-style corpus for demonstrating multi-document reasoning.
You can replace it with your own dataset.

Document A: Scientific method (summary)
The scientific method is an iterative process for building reliable knowledge about the world. It commonly
includes observation, question formulation, hypothesis generation, experimentation, measurement, analysis,
and communication of results. Replication and peer review help reduce the risk of error, bias, and
overfitting to a single observation.

Document B: Industrial organization (summary)
Organizations coordinate people, resources, and processes to achieve goals. They often trade off speed vs
quality, local optimization vs global optimization, and exploration vs exploitation. Governance structures,
incentives, and information flow influence outcomes. Systems can fail when feedback loops are delayed,
metrics are misaligned, or decision-makers lack the necessary context.

Document C: Graphs and networks (summary)
Graphs model entities (nodes) and relationships (edges). Centrality measures identify influential nodes.
Community detection partitions a graph into groups of nodes with dense connections within groups and sparse
connections between groups. Multilevel clustering can reveal coarse-to-fine structure.

Document D: Retrieval-augmented generation (summary)
RAG systems combine retrieval (finding relevant context) with generation (producing an answer). Vector
retrieval uses embeddings to match semantic similarity. Graph-based retrieval can support reasoning over
relationships, offering local neighborhood context or global community summaries. RAG quality depends on
chunking, coverage, and how the model uses citations and uncertainty.
